My Future

Education

I never really had the intention to go to graduate school, but I wanted to further my education. I don't feel that what I studied in undergrad is really where my true interests lie, and I realized when I started doing more programming after college that I may have found a calling. I'm excited to begin learning about the many intricacies of web development at Galvanize, where I believe my strengths can really shine through my work.

Career

I decided to study at Galvanize so that I will have a highly employable skillset relevant to a field that I believe will give me real career satisfaction. I'm still not sure exactly where I want to start my new career, but I have a few ideas:

  • Video streaming services like Netflix, Hulu, and YouTube are continuing to rise in popularity so much that the market is shifting away from cable television and physical DVDs. I use them every day, and I love their dynamic nature
  • Social media is quite literally revolutionizing the world, and I would love to be a part of that revolution.
  • Working for a startup would give me the opportunity to help a smaller business grow and see more of the full picture of a business.

I'm open to a lot of ideas, and I'm excited to see where my path leads.

Life

One of my long-term dreams is to live abroad and travel. I'm particularly drawn to South America and Southeast Asia. I've lived in Colorado for almost my entire life, and I would love to experience more. I love experiencing new cultures and learning new languages. I think having the skills of a web developer would provide me with a great opportunity to be able to accomplish that goal because web developers are valuable in every industry in almost every part of the world.
